Abstract

The field of federated learning (FL) has gained prominence as a promising approach for training machine learning models on distributed data while safeguarding privacy. However, the effectiveness of FL algorithms can be considerably impacted by non-independent and identically distributed (Non-IID) data across participating clients. This study offers a thorough comparative analysis of several notable FL algorithms, including FedAvg, FedProx, SCAFFOLD, FedNova, FedAdagrad/ Yogi/Adam, MOON, and FedBN, under diverse Non-IID data conditions. We examine the theoretical underpinnings of these algorithms and assess their performance using established datasets and evaluation metrics. Our findings illuminate the advantages and limitations of each algorithm in managing various types of Non-IID data, offering valuable guidance for selecting the most suitable FL algorithm for specific use cases. Additionally, we explore the constraints of current methodologies and propose avenues for future investigation.
